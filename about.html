About Women Who Code

Women Who Code is an international nonprofit organization dedicated to inspiring women to excel in technology careers and building a supportive community.

Our History

Founded in 2011 by a small team of engineers in San Francisco, Women Who Code began as a community group seeking connection and support in navigating the tech industry. The movement grew city by city, fueled by passionate volunteers inspired by our mission. In 2013, Women Who Code, Inc., was registered as a 501(c)(3) non-profit organization in California, and in 2018, we moved our headquarters to Atlanta, Georgia. Today, we have a vibrant community of over 360,000 technologists worldwide.

Our Values

We believe in creating a diverse, inclusive, and equitable tech industry. Our commitment to empowerment and support enables our members to grow, mentor each other, and navigate their careers confidently.

Community Impact

Women Who Code has made a significant impact, organizing over 20,000 community-led events, awarding more than $3.5 million in scholarships, and logging over one million volunteer hours. Our members in 145 countries share knowledge through technical talks, and our close-knit team drives day-to-day operations, all working towards a shared goal of industry diversity.
